Katch

  • Genre: Third Person Platformer
  • Engine: Unity
  • Language: C#
  • Team size: 2
  • Duration: 2 weeks
  • Role: Project lead, Programmer and 3D artist

How Katch the demo became a reality was after sitting countless nights with my friend thinking about ideas for the perfect game. We realized a game doesn't have to be perfect it has to be fun. Ideas were hard to come by but we figured out a smart way of getting simple ideas and expanding on them rather than create something that makes sense. We asked one of the other students at our school to give us a random word and he said "Hugs".

"Hugs" was the main theme the game was going to be based around. We never really managed to nail down the hugs in the game and it rather became a game of tag with a bunch of NPC's. Even though the game was terrible it was fun and entertaining even though the game had terrible controls when we considered it "finished"/"good enough" it was entertaining.

With Katch being made in early October and our school soon going on a trip to SpillExpo 2018 Norway in Lillestr√∏m we actually got the oppertunity to set up a simple computer with the game running for people to try out. It became a hit! There were almost always someone playing the game and it was awesome. Someone even called it better than Fortnite however it was most likely ironic. It was an experience I am never going to forget.
