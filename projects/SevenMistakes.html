Seven Mistakes

  • Genre: Third Person Story
  • Engine: Unity
  • Language: C#
  • Team size: 1
  • Duration: N/A
  • Role: All

The concept: A dude falls in to a forest but as he walks through it he corrupts the forest and he has a linear conversation with the developer of the game (me), He meets interesting people and solves puzzles revolving around the Seven Deadly Sins. Once he has gotten to the end of the forest he falls in to the same forest but this time the forest is the opposite color and everything is corrupted. Now you walk through the forest seeing everything you destroyed and wondering if it was worth it.

I mean the concept was cool in some ways but the game ended up not being fun and I had to move on.

One of the games mechancis would be for the camerea to be able to tilt in either direction revealing secrets or hints throughout the game to solve the specific puzzles. Or even reveal lore. The game did change from 3D to a 2D orthographic view within a 3D world so that made the game mechanic possible.
